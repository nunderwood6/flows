Enduring Connections: Visualizing U.S.-Guatemalan Remittances

Of the more than 1.8 million remittance-sending Guatemalans, over 1.3 million are undocumented. Over 30% of the population in Guatemala depends on this crucial economic lifeline, including more than 2 million kids receiving remittances from parents. This money enables many to attend school: the average number of years of schooling for the population receiving remittances- 15 years of age and over - is 8.4, higher than the national average that reaches 4.9 years (INE, 2011).
